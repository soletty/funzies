## 1. The Foundational Split: Adnan vs Qahtan

All Arabian tribal genealogy traces back to two legendary ancestors:

### Qahtanites (Southern Arabs / "Pure Arabs" / al-Arab al-Ariba)
- **Origin**: South Arabia (modern Yemen, Hadhramaut)
- **Ancestor**: Qahtan (identified by some with the biblical Joktan)
- **Key sub-groups**: Himyar (settled south) and Kahlan (nomadic kinsmen)
- **Kahlan divisions**: Tayy, Azd (invaded Oman), Amila-Judham (Palestine), Hamdan-Madhhij (remained in Yemen)
- **Gulf tribes of Qahtani origin**: Qahtan (tribe), parts of Azd-descended groups in Oman/UAE
- **Wikipedia**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qahtanite

### Adnanites (Northern Arabs / "Arabized Arabs" / al-Arab al-Mustarabah)
- **Origin**: Hejaz, Central/North Arabia
- **Ancestor**: Adnan, descended from Ishmael (son of Abraham)
- **Key branches**: Mudar, Rabi'ah, Qays Aylan
- **The Prophet Muhammad** belonged to the Quraysh tribe of the Adnanites
- **Gulf tribes of Adnani origin**: Bani Yas, Anizzah (from which Al Saud, Al Khalifa, Al Sabah descend), Bani Tamim (Al Thani), Otaiba, Harb, Shammar, Mutayr
- **Wikipedia**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adnanites

### Why This Matters
When someone says "we are Qahtani" or "we are Adnani," they are making a claim about the deepest roots of their tribal identity — southern/Yemeni origin vs. northern/Hijazi origin. This is the first branching point in any tribal genealogy tool.

## 2. UAE — Tribal Map by Emirate

### Complete List of UAE Tribes
Awamir, Al-Awazim, Bani Hadiyah, Bani Kaab, Bani Qitab, Bani Shatair, Bani Yas, Dahaminah, Daramikah, Dawasir, Al-Dhafeer, Dhawahir, Duru, Ghafalah, Habus, Harb, Al Bu Hamir, Mahamid, Bani Hajer, Khawatir, Kunud, Mahris, Manasir, Marar, Maraziq, Mazari, Al Bu Muhair, Na'im, Naqbiyin, Al Nuaimi, Otaibah, Qahtan, Al Qasimi, Rashid, Al Bu Shamis, Sharqiyin, Shihuh, Sudan, Tunaij, Zaab.

### ABU DHABI — Bani Yas Confederation

**The Bani Yas** is the dominant tribal confederation, consisting of ~20 subsections. Originated in Najd (Central Arabia), descended from Yas ibn Amer ibn Sa'sa'a. Adnanite lineage.

**Ruling Family: Al Nahyan** (branch of Al Bu Falah)
- Al Bu Falah held the most power in the confederation
- Settled in Liwa oasis circa 1700
- Migrated to Abu Dhabi island in 1793 after discovery of fresh water
- Sheikh Shakhbut bin Dhiyab Al Nahyan established Abu Dhabi as political center in 1795
- Current ruler: Sheikh Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan (also UAE President)

**Key Bani Yas Sub-sections:**
- **Al Bu Falah** — Ruling section, gave rise to Al Nahyan family
- **Al Bu Falasah** — Dubai's ruling section, gave rise to Al Maktoum family; split in 1833
- **Rumaithat** — Fishing & pearling, coastal tribe
- **Al Bu Mahair** — Fishing & pearling, coastal tribe
- **Qubaisat** — One of the largest, settled in Liwa oasis
- **Mazrui** — Main Bedouin section, nomadic
- **Hawamil** — Semi-settled, permanent population of Liwa
- **Sudan** — Western region, rising political influence under Sheikh Zayed
- **Marar** — Allied sub-tribe
- **Qubaisi** — Allied sub-tribe
- **Remeithi** — Allied sub-tribe

**Major Allied Tribes in Abu Dhabi:**
- **Dhawahir** — Buraimi/Al Ain, long alliance with Al Nahyan; rival of Na'im tribe
- **Manasir** — Liwa/Al Gharbia, biggest tribe in emirate; tradition that one section was once Christian
- **Awamir** — Desert nomads, camel breeders, roamed Rub al-Khali
- **Bani Kaab** — Fringe of Trucial Coast, sub-sections: Drisah, Makatim, Misaid, Shwaihiyin

### DUBAI — Al Bu Falasah (Bani Yas offshoot)

**Ruling Family: Al Maktoum** (branch of Al Bu Falasah)
- In 1833, Maktoum bin Butti led ~800 Bani Yas members to settle Dubai
- Split from Abu Dhabi due to internal factional disputes
- Current ruler: Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum (also UAE Vice President & PM)

### SHARJAH — Al Qasimi (Qawasim) Confederation

**Ruling Family: Al Qasimi**
- Origins debated: possibly Najd or among Huwala groups from the Persian coast
- Part of the Sunni Huwalah confederation
- First historical records circa 1624
- Rose to power after decline of Oman's Ya'rubid dynasty in early 18th century
- Became dominant maritime power, controlling ports on both sides of the Persian Gulf
- Also ruled Lengeh on the Persian coast until Iran annexed it in 1887
- British branded them "pirates" — contested by current ruler Sultan bin Mohammed
- Current ruler: Sheikh Sultan bin Muhammad Al-Qasimi (since 1972)
- **Key fact**: The Al Qasimi are the ONLY royal family to rule TWO emirates (Sharjah and Ras Al Khaimah)

### RAS AL KHAIMAH — Al Qasimi (separate branch)
- Split from Sharjah in 1869, temporarily reincorporated 1900, independent again 1921
- Was the LAST emirate to join the UAE (February 10, 1972)

### AJMAN — Na'im Tribe

**Ruling Family: Al Nuaimi** (from Al Bu Kharaiban section of Na'im)
- Na'im is a major Ghafiri tribal confederation: Al Bu Kharaiban, Khawatir, Al Bu Shamis
- Founded Ajman circa 1816-1817
- Current ruler: Sheikh Humaid bin Rashid Al Nuaimi III (since 1981)

### UMM AL QUWAIN — Al Ali Tribe

**Ruling Family: Al Mualla** (head of Al Ali tribe)
- Originated in Nejd
- First settled on Siniyah island, then moved to mainland
- Founded emirate in 1768
- Current ruler: Sheikh Saud bin Rashid Al Mualla

### FUJAIRAH — Sharqiyin Tribe

**Ruling Family: Al Sharqi** (head of Sharqiyin tribe)
- Name means "Easterners" — dominant tribe of the east coast
- Originally dependents of Sharjah; repeatedly tried to secede
- 1952: Finally gained British recognition as a Trucial State (last to be recognized)
- Current ruler: Sheikh Hamad bin Mohammed Al Sharqi (since 1974)

### Ghafiri vs Hinawi — The Great Tribal Alignment
- **Ghafiri**: Al Qasimi, Na'im, Dhawahir, Bani Kaab, Bani Qitab (generally northern/interior)
- **Hinawi**: Bani Yas, Sharqiyin, parts of east coast tribes (generally coastal/southern)
- Originated from the Omani civil war of the early 18th century

## 3. Bahrain — Tribes, Ethnic Groups & Families

### Ruling Family: Al Khalifa
- Part of the **Bani Utbah** confederation (same as Kuwait's Al Sabah)
- Trace lineage to the **Anizzah** tribe
- Migration path: Umm Qasr (Central Arabia) → Kuwait → Zubarah (Qatar, 1760s) → Conquered Bahrain from Persians in 1783

### Key Bahraini Tribal Families
Al Khalifa, Al-Ma'awdah, Al-Buainain, Al-Fadhil, Al-Kuwari, Al-Mannai, Al-Noaimi, Al-Rumaihi, Al-Sulaiti, Al-Thawadi, Banu Abdul Qays, Bani Tamim

### The Baharna — Indigenous Shia Arabs
- Considered original inhabitants of Eastern Arabia
- Pre-Islamic roots: mix of partially-Christianized Arabs, Aramaic-speaking agriculturalists, Persian Zoroastrians, and some Jews
- Location: Manama, most villages on main island, Muharraq, Sitra
- Traditional economy: Palm farming and fishing
- Key fact: They were there BEFORE the Bani Utbah (Al Khalifa) arrived

### The Ajam — Shia Persians
- Bahraini citizens of Iranian/Persian descent, mostly Shia
- Migrated mainly from Bushehr and Fars province (1920-1940)
- Estimated ~100,000+, about 14% of population

### The Huwala — Sunni Arabs Who Returned from Persia
- Arabs who migrated TO the Persian coast (1500s-1700s), then BACK to Arabia (late 1800s-1930s)
- Include tribes: Qawasem, Hammadi, Al Nasur, Obaidli, Bani Tamim

### Jewish Families of Bahrain
- ~37 people today, down from ~600 pre-1948
- Key families: Nonoo, Cohen, Rouben, Khedouri
- Houda Nonoo was Ambassador to the United States (2008-2013) — first Jewish ambassador from an Arab country

### Bahrain's Ethnic Composition
- Sunni Arabs (most powerful, ruling family)
- Baharna (Shia Arab, indigenous)
- Ajam (Shia, Persian)
- Huwala (Sunni, Arab-Persian mix)
- Baloch (Sunni, from Balochistan)
- Afro-Bahrainis (historical slave trade legacy)
- Jews (~37 remaining)

## 4. Qatar — Tribes & Ruling Dynasty

### Ruling Family: Al Thani (House of Thani)
- Branch of the **Maadhid tribe**, claim descent from **Bani Tamim**
- Originally from **Jabrin oasis** in southern Nejd
- Migration: Jabrin → south Qatar → Zubarah → Fuwairit → Doha (1848-1850)
- Three main branches: Bani Ali, Bani Hamad, Bani Khalid
- Ruling dynasty since 1847

### Other Qatari Families
Al-Kuwari, Al-Mannai, Al-Sulaiti, Al-Buainain, Al Attiyah, Al Naimi, Al Murrah, Al Dosari

## 5. Saudi Arabia — Major Tribal Confederations

### The House of Saud
- Descended from Banu Hanifa, connected to the Anizzah confederation
- Adnanite lineage

### Major Saudi Tribes
- **Anizzah** — Adnanite, one of the largest; Al Saud, Al Khalifa, Al Sabah all descend from sub-branches
- **Shammar** — Qahtanite (from Tayy), northern Arabia, rivals of Al Saud
- **Otaiba/Utaybah** — Adnanite, one of the biggest
- **Qahtan** — Qahtanite, southern Saudi
- **Harb** — Hejaz, very large
- **Dawasir** — Wadi Dawasir, spread to Bahrain/Kuwait
- **Mutayr** — Adnanite, central Arabia
- **Al Murrah** — Qahtanite, Empty Quarter/Eastern Province
- **Bani Tamim** — Adnanite, Al Thani of Qatar are a branch
- **Subay'** — Adnanite, Najd
- **Banu Khalid** — Adnanite, once controlled Eastern Arabia
- **Ajman** (tribe) — Qahtanite, NOT the emirate
- **Zahran** — Qahtanite (Azd), Al-Baha
- **Ghamid** — Qahtanite (Azd), Al-Baha

## 6. Cross-Border Tribal Connections

### The Anizzah Super-Tribe
- Saudi Arabia: Al Saud, Kuwait: Al Sabah, Bahrain: Al Khalifa — all connected through Anizzah

### The Bani Utbah Confederation
- Al Khalifa (Bahrain) and Al Sabah (Kuwait) descend from this group
- Also includes Al Jalahma

### Key cross-border tribes:
- Bani Tamim: Saudi, Qatar, UAE, Iraq, Kuwait
- Dawasir: Saudi, Bahrain, Kuwait
- Al Murrah: Saudi, Qatar
- Na'im: UAE, Oman, Bahrain
- Baloch: UAE (~468,000), Bahrain, Oman, Kuwait

## 7. Key Historical Events

1. **Federation proposal (1968-1971)**: Nine sheikhdoms considered forming one federation. Bahrain and Qatar withdrew.
2. **UAE formation (December 2, 1971)**: Seven emirates, RAK joining February 10, 1972
3. **1972 Sharjah coup**: Saqr bin Sultan attempted to retake Sharjah from Khalid. 22-year-old Mohammed bin Rashid led troops to end it. Sultan bin Muhammad became ruler.
4. **1987 Sharjah coup attempt**: Sheikh Abd al-Aziz attempted bloodless coup. Four-day standoff. Federal Supreme Council brokered deal.
5. **1972 Qatar coup**: Khalifa deposed his cousin Ahmad. Khalifa himself was deposed by his own son Hamad in 1995.
6. **British-Qawasim "Pirate Wars" (1809, 1819)**: Led to 1820 treaty and "Trucial Coast" naming.
7. **Iran's seizure of Tunb Islands (1971)**: Convinced RAK to join UAE.

## 8. Ethnic & Cultural Nuances

- "Emirati" doesn't mean one ethnicity — includes Arab, Persian, Baloch, African, Huwala origins
- The Huwala Paradox: Persian-sounding names but ethnically Arab
- Manasir Christian tradition: one section was once Christian
- Baharna vs. ruling family tension
- "Al" prefix means "Family of" (آل), different from "al-" definite article (الـ)
- Family names reveal origin: Al-Balushi=Baloch, Al-Farsi=Persian, Al-Hindi=Indian

[Given Name] bin/bint [Father's Name] bin [Grandfather's Name] [Family/Tribal Name]
